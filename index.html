Sense-Mind

Sensor-based Environmental & Mental Interaction Study

The study was supported from the SPARC (Seed Projects in Arts and Sciences Research and Creative Activities) program in the College of Arts and Sciences at University at Buffalo. This project aims to examine the impact of air pollution, extreme heat, and greenspace on mental health, exploring disparities and psychosocial mechanisms through retrospective and prospective studies in order to find ways to reduce the negative effects.

The Details

What is the goal of this study?

Our study explores how the environment we live in—such as the air we breathe and the temperature around us—can influence how we feel and function.

Why participate?

By joining this study, you’ll help us learn how air quality, temperature, and other environmental conditions affect mood and mental well-being. Your participation will support research that can lead to healthier, more resilient communities and guide the design of cities that promote mental health for everyone.

Become a participant!

If you are interested in hosting an air quality sensor outside your home or business, or if you would like more information, please contact ubairqualitystudyinfo@gmail.com.
